Bellaoud Elmehdi

just a regular 18-year-old dude whose love for snapping pics started with his grandpa's old-school camera vibes. Like, you know, hearing the old man's stories and flipping through those vintage photo albums was legit inspiring. Got hold of that antique camera, dusted it off, and bam! Mehdi's photographic journey began. From exploring the attic filled with forgotten snaps to raiding the family albums, it was like diving headfirst into a time machine. Each click of that vintage shutter felt like tapping into a family legacy, turning pixels into memories. Spent hours figuring out the whole camera mojo, making rookie mistakes, and learning the ropes. Mehdi's lens turned into a storyteller, capturing the vibe of everyday life. It was like painting pictures with light and shadow, freezing moments in time that screamed emotions. From a total noob to a camera-wielding storyteller, Mehdi's skills evolved. He's not just taking pics; he's carrying on his grandpa's legacy. Clicking that shutter isn't just a snap; it's a beat that ties generations together through the timeless art of photography.
